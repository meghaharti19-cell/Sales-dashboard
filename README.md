# BlinkIt Sales Performance Analysis

## 1. Project Description
This project aims to provide a deep-dive analysis of **BlinkIt’s sales performance** using historical retail data. The goal is to evaluate various factors influencing grocery sales, such as item attributes (fat content, visibility, type) and outlet characteristics (size, location, establishment year). 

By transforming raw data into an interactive Power BI dashboard, this project enables stakeholders to identify trends, optimize inventory distribution, and discover key drivers of revenue.

### Key Objectives:
* Analyze revenue trends over time and across different outlet categories.
* Identify the most profitable product types and customer preferences regarding fat content.
* Evaluate the impact of outlet size and location (Tier 1, 2, 3) on total sales.
* Assess customer satisfaction through rating distributions.

---

## 2. Dataset Description
The analysis is based on a dataset containing **8,523 records** and **12 key attributes**:
* **Item Fat Content:** Whether the product is Low Fat or Regular.
* **Item Identifier:** Unique ID for each product.
* **Item Type:** Category of the product (e.g., Fruits, Snacks, Household).
* **Outlet Establishment Year:** The year the store was opened.
* **Outlet Identifier:** Unique ID for the retail outlet.
* **Outlet Location Type:** Classification of the city (Tier 1, Tier 2, Tier 3).
* **Outlet Size:** Size of the store (Small, Medium, High).
* **Outlet Type:** Type of retail setup (Supermarket Type 1/2/3, Grocery Store).
* **Item Visibility:** The percentage of total display area allocated to a product.
* **Item Weight:** Physical weight of the product.
* **Sales:** Revenue generated by the item.
* **Rating:** Average customer rating for the item.

---

## 3. Business Problem
BlinkIt needs to understand how different outlet configurations and product features affect their bottom line. The business seeks answers to:
1.  Which outlet types generate the maximum revenue?
2.  How does item visibility correlate with sales?
3.  Is there a significant difference in sales between Low Fat and Regular products?
4.  Which geographic tiers (Tiers 1-3) should be prioritized for future expansion?

---

## 4. Data Cleaning & Transformation
To ensure accuracy, the following steps were performed in **Power Query**:
* **Data Standardizing:** Fixed inconsistent naming in `Item Fat Content` (e.g., changing 'LF' and 'low fat' to 'Low Fat').
* **Handling Nulls:** Addressed missing values in `Item Weight` and `Outlet Size` using conditional imputation based on `Item Type`.
* **Feature Engineering:** Calculated Total Sales and Average Sales using DAX measures.
* **Data Type Formatting:** Ensured numeric columns were correctly typed for aggregation.

---

## 5. Analysis Steps
1.  **KPI Identification:** Created core metrics: Total Sales ($1.2M), Average Sales ($141), Total Items (8,523), and Average Rating (3.9).
2.  **Comparative Analysis:** Used Donut charts to compare Fat Content and Funnel charts to analyze sales by Outlet Size.
3.  **Trend Analysis:** Employed Line charts to visualize sales growth based on Outlet Establishment years.
4.  **Categorical Analysis:** Ranked Item Types (Fruits/Vegetables, Snacks) using Bar charts to see which categories dominate the market.
5.  **Interactive Filtering:** Integrated Slicers for Outlet Location, Outlet Size, and Item Type for dynamic reporting.

---

## 6. Data Insights
* **Top Sellers:** Fruits and Vegetables ($178K) and Snack Foods ($175K) are the primary revenue drivers.
* **Preference:** Customers prefer **Low Fat** items, contributing to roughly 64% of total sales.
* **Top Performer:** **Tier 3** locations and **Medium-sized** outlets yield the highest sales figures.
* **Outlet Type:** **Supermarket Type 1** accounts for the majority of the business volume compared to other formats.

---

## 7. Recommendations
* **Inventory Shift:** Increase the variety of Low Fat options within the Snack and Dairy categories.
* **Expansion Strategy:** Focus new outlet openings in **Tier 3** cities using the **Medium-sized** Supermarket Type 1 model.
* **Visibility Optimization:** Improve the "Item Visibility" for underperforming categories like Seafood and Breakfast items to boost their conversion rates.
* **Rating Focus:** Items with ratings below 3.0 should be reviewed for quality issues or pricing adjustments.
